Media Players
11/12/20

Blog Audio Book: (Audio may be loud by default. Turn it down before listening.)

The lab from today for finding various artists and albums made me realize that I have never tried to implement audio or video into a webpage. If we gave the objects in the lab a file name, we could implement a system that would retrieve the requested song or album to the user that they would then be able to listen to. The same would apply to videos.

This made me think of what platforms online use a system that may be similar to this. This article contains a list of many popular websites that are built with React. Facebook, Instagram, and Netflix are the biggest household names on the list and I wonder if their search bars are made in a similar fashion to today's lab. Also, many of these websites will use very different media players than the default HTML player.

Thankfully, media players can be very simple in HTML. There are already tools to include files such as mp3 and mp4 in a playable fashion on a webpage with the audio and video tags. They are also super easy to use. Both audio and video formats follow a very similar style and just need an appropriate file format to read from.
